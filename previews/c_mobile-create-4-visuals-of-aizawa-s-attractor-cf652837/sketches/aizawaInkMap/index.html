Aizawa Ink Map projects multiple settled Aizawa trajectories onto a 2D plane and interprets visitation density as contour ink, producing topographic bands rather than a literal 3D object.

Variable meanings

The dynamics remain 3D, but rendering evaluates radial distance in screen space to form scalar fields ρ\rhoρ and κ\kappaκ that map naturally to paper/ink tones.

Exposition

By layering three burned-in seeds, the map captures recurrent neighborhoods of the attractor as darker basins and contour bands. This turns chaotic recurrence into something closer to a cartographic print.
