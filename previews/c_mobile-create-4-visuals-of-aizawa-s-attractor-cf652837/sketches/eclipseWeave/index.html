Eclipse Weave now places the viewer between two receding planes: an earthy floor and a luminous sky ceiling, both fading into atmospheric haze at depth.

What is happening

A perspective-style depth map is built from distance to horizon, then used to sample separate procedural worlds for ground and sky. Both worlds are gradually obscured by fog as they recede.

Key variables

Horizon offset gain (0.08)(0.08)(0.08), depth denominator bias (0.035)(0.035)(0.035), and haze window (2,18)(2,18)(2,18) most strongly control camera feel, vanishing-point compression, and atmospheric distance.
